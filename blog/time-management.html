What is agile? — Developing a new project without the drama

With , from Trevor

Lets go!

When starting a new project, especially when working in a team, there are many things that need to go just right to avoid the dreaded “drama”. You know the type -- the constant change in design, scope, and requirements; not knowing what pieces to start first or work on next -- that "drama".

Say hello to a development method called "agile". It's a method that includes 5 stages to better manage the project and working with team members.

Without getting into the details too much, here are the 5 stages:

1) Planning - Define the purpose & goal
2) Development - First stage of building
3) Testing - Addressing bugs & errors
4) Deployment - Ready for release
5) Operations - Continuous maintainance

Agile processes defer decisions, shorten development cycles, and support just in time analysis of requests. This allows agile teams to change quickly and at a low cost. That provides a competitive advantage and is one of the keys to agile ways of working.
